Abstract

Robotic manipulation policies are typically reactive (conditioning only on recent observations) or depend on explicit planning and state estimation. Many household tasks, however, rely on anticipating near-future events such as contact timing and articulated motion. We study control under imperfect future supervision: policies are trained with a future signal available from demonstrations, but at inference time we don't have access to ground-truth information so the future data must be provided by a generative model and may be time-shifted or slightly mismatched. We propose Future-Experience Conditioning (FEC), a learning formulation that conditions closed-loop control on a compact latent representation of a short-horizon executable visual future while explicitly training for temporal misalignment. We instantiate FEC with (i) a robot-free digital-twin (DT) articulation rollout that specifies target object motion, (ii) a mask-free video diffusion model (VDM) that inpaints the robot into the DT video to produce a task-consistent future clip without requiring segmentation at inference, and (iii) a Streaming Flow Policy (SFP) that consumes recent observations and an aligned, temporally-binned future latent. We evaluated our framework on Robocasa and Calvin benchmark to prove its capability on different scene and environment.(To make sure) Experiments evaluate FEC on long-horizon manipulation and quantify robustness to future timing mismatch as well as the train and test swap from ground-truth futures to generated futures.

Approach

ENACT as an instantiation of Future-Experience Conditioning (FEC): DT rollout → mask-free diffusion robot inpainting → future tokens → policy conditioning (robust to temporal shift).

We study control under imperfect future supervision, where the future used during policy training (from demonstrations) differs from the future available at deployment (generated) and may be temporally misaligned. ENACT produces a short-horizon future experience clip and conditions a closed-loop policy on a compact latent representation of that future while explicitly training for temporal mismatch.

Digital Twin (DT) rollout (robot-free)

We obtain the future clip by rolling out a robot-free digital twin (DT) for the intended object motion. The DT video constrains the correct articulation dynamics and camera viewpoint without requiring robot kinematics.

Mask-free video diffusion model (VDM) for robot inpainting

A mask-free video diffusion model inpaints the robot into the DT rollout to produce a task-consistent future clip without requiring robot segmentation at inference time.

Future-Experience Conditioning (FEC) tokens (temporal bins + projector)

We encode the future clip into per-frame embeddings, select a short future window, compress it into coarse temporal bins, and project it to a fixed-dimensional conditioning vector. During policy training, we apply temporal shift augmentation to make the controller robust to imperfect / time-shifted future supervision.

Future-conditioned Streaming Flow Policy (SFP)

The policy consumes recent observations together with the binned future latent and outputs continuous actions. We report ablations that swap the future source at test time (ground-truth vs generated) and quantify robustness to timing mismatch.
