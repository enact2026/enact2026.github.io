ENACT: Executable Neural Action from Video Futures

Future-conditioned closed-loop control trained to be robust to imperfect / temporally-misaligned future supervision.

Mohammad Khoshnazar · Andrew Melnik

Abstract

ENACT studies control under imperfect future supervision: a policy can be trained with a future visual signal from demonstrations, but at deployment the future must come from a generative model and may be slightly mismatched or time-shifted. We propose Future-Experience Conditioning (FEC), which conditions a closed-loop controller on a compact latent representation of a short-horizon executable future while explicitly training for temporal misalignment.

Approach

Place your method figure here (optional). Example: fig_enact_overview.png

We instantiate FEC using a robot-free digital twin (DT) articulation video that specifies object motion without requiring robot kinematics, and a mask-free video diffusion model (VDM) that inpaints a robot into the DT rollout to produce a task-consistent future observation clip. The generated clip is encoded into latent vectors and fed to a policy alongside recent observations.

1) Digital-twin rollout (robot-free)

  • Render a short-horizon “future” clip that captures object/articulation evolution without the robot.
  • This clip represents the task progress signal (what should happen), but not the robot motion (how).

2) Mask-free robot inpainting (VDM)

  • Inpaint the robot into the DT future to obtain a robot-ready future observation sequence.
  • No segmentation masks needed at inference (mask-free conditioning).

3) Future tokens (temporal binning)

  • Encode the generated future frames into latent tokens.
  • Compress them with temporal bins + a learned projector to a fixed-size conditioning vector.

4) Closed-loop policy (robust to temporal shift)

  • Policy input = recent observations + the binned future latent.
  • Train with explicit temporal shift augmentation so the controller tolerates misalignment at deployment.
