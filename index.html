ENACT: Executable Neural Action from Video Futures

Mohammad Khoshnazar · Andrew Melnik
University of Bremen · Institute of Artificial Intelligence
arXiv Paper Code Results BibTeX
ENACT studies control under imperfect future supervision via Future-Experience Conditioning (FEC): DT-guided mask-free video diffusion futures + a future-conditioned Streaming Flow Policy robust to temporal misalignment.

Abstract

Robotic manipulation policies are typically reactive (conditioning only on recent observations) or depend on explicit planning and state estimation. Many household tasks, however, rely on anticipating near-future events such as contact timing and articulated motion. We study control under imperfect future supervision: policies are trained with a future signal available from demonstrations, but at inference time we don't have access to ground-truth information so the future data must be provided by a generative model and may be time-shifted or slightly mismatched. We propose Future-Experience Conditioning (FEC), a learning formulation that conditions closed-loop control on a compact latent representation of a short-horizon executable visual future while explicitly training for temporal misalignment. We instantiate FEC with (i) a robot-free digital-twin (DT) articulation rollout that specifies target object motion, (ii) a mask-free video diffusion model (VDM) that inpaints the robot into the DT video to produce a task-consistent future clip without requiring segmentation at inference, and (iii) a Streaming Flow Policy (SFP) that consumes recent observations and an aligned, temporally-binned future latent. We evaluated our framework on Robocasa and Calvin benchmark to prove its capability on different scene and environment. Experiments evaluate FEC on long-horizon manipulation and quantify robustness to future timing mismatch as well as the train and test swap from ground-truth futures to generated futures.

Step-by-step ENACT pipeline

1. Build the future The command and current observation define the intended object motion, then DT rollout generates a robot-free future.
2. Make it executable Mask-free VDM inpaints the robot into the transparent DT video to create a task-consistent future clip.
3. Compress the future The future clip is encoded, pooled into temporal bins, and projected into a compact future-conditioning vector.
4. Use it in closed loop Policy consumes recent observations and the future latent to generate actions robustly even under timing mismatch.

Approach

We study control under imperfect future supervision, where the future used during policy training differs from the future available at deployment and may be temporally misaligned. ENACT produces a short-horizon future experience clip and conditions a closed-loop controller on a compact latent representation of that future while explicitly training for temporal mismatch.

Digital Twin (DT) rollout

ENACT first generates a robot-free digital twin rollout that specifies the intended object motion and articulation trajectory. This transparent future constrains what should happen in the scene without requiring robot kinematics.

Mask-free robot inpainting with video diffusion

A mask-free video diffusion model then inpaints the robot into the DT rollout to produce a task-consistent future clip. This makes the future visually executable while avoiding robot segmentation at inference time.

Future-Experience Conditioning (FEC)

The generated future is encoded into per-frame embeddings, compressed into temporal bins, and projected to a fixed-dimensional future latent. During training, temporal shift augmentation is applied so the policy remains robust when the future is slightly early, late, shorter, or longer than the true execution.

Future-conditioned Streaming Flow Policy (SFP)

The policy consumes recent observations together with the future latent and produces continuous actions in closed loop. This enables controlled comparisons between no-future, ground-truth future, and generated future conditioning.
