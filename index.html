Overview

ENACT studies control under imperfect future supervision: a policy can be trained with a future visual signal from demonstrations, but at deployment the future must come from a generative model and may be slightly mismatched or time-shifted.

We propose Future-Experience Conditioning (FEC), which conditions a closed-loop controller on a compact latent representation of a short-horizon executable future while explicitly training for temporal misalignment.

Method

(Short, project-page version)

  • DT rollout: generate a robot-free “transparent” articulation clip that specifies object motion.
  • Mask-free inpainting (VDM): inpaint the robot into the DT clip to produce an executable future without masks at inference.
  • FEC future tokens: encode future frames, compress with temporal bins + a learned projector to a fixed-size conditioning vector.
  • Policy: a closed-loop controller consumes recent observations + the binned future latent (robust to temporal shift).
